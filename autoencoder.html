Autoencoder reconstruction:

Random Latent Vector (Autoencoder as Generator)

Here latent vectors are sampled randomly from a multivariate Normal distribution fit to the distribution of data latent vectors.

Due to sampling from a fitted Normal distribution, we are in the same region of latent space as the encoded data, but even so, generating new images of the ball is hit-or-miss: many latent vectors decode to something that does not look like a ball. This means that the latent vectors that do decode to valid balls is sparse in latent space. With higher-dimensional latent spaces, the manifold gets sparser.

Visualize the latent space in 2D

In[]:
# Switch model to evaluation mode
autoencoder.eval()

# Get latent space representations
latent_space_representations = []
labels = []

with torch.no_grad():
    for data, target in test_loader:
        data = data.to(device)
        encoded_data = autoencoder.encoder(data).cpu().numpy()
        latent_space_representations.append(encoded_data)
        labels.append(target.numpy())

latent_space_representations = np.concatenate(latent_space_representations, axis=0)
labels = np.concatenate(labels, axis=0)
In[]:
from sklearn.decomposition import PCA

# Apply PCA to reduce the dimensionality to 2D
pca = PCA(n_components=2)
pca_result = pca.fit_transform(latent_space_representations)

# Plot the 2D embeddings
scatter = plt.scatter(pca_result[:, 0], pca_result[:, 1], c=labels, cmap='tab10', s=20)
plt.colorbar(scatter)
plt.title('PCA Visualization of Embeddings')
plt.show()
